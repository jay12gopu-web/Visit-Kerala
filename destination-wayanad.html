Wayanad

Ancient rock art, forest roads, wildlife, waterfalls, paddy valleys, plantations, and living Adivasi culture.

Why Wayanad

Kerala at its wild, layered northern edge.

Wayanad brings together highland forests, rice fields, coffee and spice plantations, wildlife habitats, and traces of human life stretching back to the New Stone Age. Its name is commonly linked to Vayal Nadu, the land of paddy fields.

Famous Places

What To See In Wayanad

Prehistory

Edakkal Caves

Climb to the rock shelter to see engravings that make Wayanad one of Kerala's most important windows into prehistoric human life.

Scenery

Pookode Lake & Lakkidi

Combine a calm freshwater lake with the hairpin roads and broad Western Ghats views around Wayanad's mountain gateway.

Wild Wayanad

Waterfalls & Wildlife

Build a day around forest landscapes, seasonal waterfalls, and the Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary. Access depends on weather and local rules.

The Story

Stone-age traces and a resilient highland culture.

Archaeological evidence points to organised human life in Wayanad from the New Stone Age, with the Edakkal engravings preserving its best-known early record. The district's story also includes long-established Adivasi communities and periods under the Kottayam dynasty, Mysore rulers, and the British, each leaving marks on the region's land and society.

Taste Wayanad

Highland staples and plantation flavour.

  • Locally grown coffee and spice-infused tea
  • Puttu with kadala curry
  • Appam with stew or rich meat curry
  • Kerala fish curry brightened with local spices
Build It In

Keep two full days for the highlands.

Wayanad sits away from Kerala's classic Kochi-to-coast route, so it works best in the 10-day plan, where road time and weather can be handled without rushing.
