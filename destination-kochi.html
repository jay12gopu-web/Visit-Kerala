Fort Kochi & Mattancherry

A walkable meeting point of Kerala, Arab, Jewish, Portuguese, Dutch, British, and contemporary coastal culture.

Why Kochi

Kerala's layered gateway city.

Fort Kochi and Mattancherry are where many Kerala journeys begin. Heritage buildings, waterfront trade, places of worship, art spaces, spice shops, and cafes sit close enough to explore on foot and by ferry.

The Story

A port shaped by centuries of exchange.

Fort Kochi's history reflects maritime trade and successive Portuguese, Dutch, and British control. Portuguese influence began in the early sixteenth century, the Dutch took Kochi in 1663, and British power followed in the eighteenth century. Mattancherry developed alongside it as a commercial and multicultural quarter tied to spices and the sea.
