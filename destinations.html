Where To Go

Kerala Destinations

Grouped by Kerala Tourism's core travel themes: hills, backwaters, beaches, wildlife, culture, and heritage streets.

Hills

Munnar

Munnar is Kerala's signature hill-station escape, known for tea plantations, cool weather, valley views, and slow scenic drives through Idukki.

  • Tea estate walks and viewpoints
  • Works well in 5-day and 7-day plans
Discover Munnar
Backwaters

Alappuzha

Alappuzha is the classic houseboat base for Kerala's backwater routes, with canals, paddy fields, coconut groves, village life, and quiet sunset cruises.

  • Houseboat cruise highlight
  • Best for first-time Kerala visitors
Discover Alappuzha
Lake

Kumarakom

Set along Vembanad Lake, Kumarakom is a peaceful backwater alternative with birding, resorts, houseboats, and wellness stays.

  • Ideal for relaxed couples and families
  • Easy to combine with Kochi
Discover Kumarakom
Beach

Varkala & Kovalam

Kerala Tourism highlights Kovalam and Varkala as popular beach choices. Varkala brings red cliffs and sea views; Kovalam offers a classic resort-beach finish.

  • Strong ending for 7-day vacations
  • Add Ayurveda and seafood stops
Discover the coast
Forest

Wayanad

Wayanad adds forests, waterfalls, plantations, ancient caves, and a greener north Kerala rhythm to longer trips.

  • Best in 10-day Kerala plan
  • Nature, trekking, and highland stays
Discover Wayanad
Heritage

Fort Kochi & Mattancherry

Kerala Tourism's FAQ points to Fort Kochi and Mattancherry for culture, history, shopping, heritage streets, and the beginning or end of many routes.

  • Chinese fishing nets and palace quarter
  • Great first-day arrival base
Discover Kochi
