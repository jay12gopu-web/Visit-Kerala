Munnar

Tea-covered hills, cool air, wildlife, waterfalls, and long scenic roads through Kerala's Western Ghats.

Why Munnar

Kerala's best-known hill escape.

Munnar sits around 1,600 metres above sea level and rewards a slower stay. Plantation roads curl between tea gardens, forest edges, reservoirs, and viewpoints, making it a natural contrast to Kochi and the backwaters.

Famous Places

What To See In Munnar

Wildlife

Eravikulam National Park

High grasslands, mountain views, and the protected habitat associated with the Nilgiri tahr. Entry and access can vary, so check current conditions.

Plantation

Tea Estates & Museum

Walk through tea country and learn how plantation life and tea production shaped modern Munnar.

Scenic Drive

Mattupetty & Kundala

Reservoir views, boating, winding roads, and easy stops that work well in a relaxed half-day circuit.

The Story

From Muthuvan homeland to tea country.

The region's early history is closely connected with the Muthuvan community. Munnar's plantation era expanded in the late nineteenth century as the Kannan Devan Hills were leased and developed for commercial crops. Tea took hold in the 1880s and came to define the landscape, economy, and visual identity visitors know today.

Taste Munnar

Warm food for cool hills.

  • Fresh local tea and cardamom chai
  • Appam with vegetable or chicken stew
  • Kerala meals fragrant with hill-grown spices
  • Tea-shop snacks such as pazham pori
Build It In

Give Munnar two unhurried days.
