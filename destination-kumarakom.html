Vembanad Backwaters

Kumarakom

Lake mornings, mangrove-lined canals, village life, birdwatching, and slow journeys across Vembanad.

Why Kumarakom

A quieter window into the backwaters.

Kumarakom is a cluster of small islands on Vembanad Lake, about 14 kilometres from Kottayam. Its waterways, bird habitat, farms, and village experiences make it ideal for travellers who want the backwaters at an easy pace.

Famous Places

What To See In Kumarakom

Backwaters

Vembanad Lake

Take a canoe or houseboat across Kerala's longest lake, watching fishing boats, coconut groves, and waterside villages pass by.

Nature

Bird Sanctuary

This compact wetland sanctuary is known for resident and migratory birds. Early morning is best for a quiet walk or guided boat visit.

Local Life

Village Experiences

Meet local makers, see coir and coconut-based livelihoods, visit farms, and experience the community-led side of Kumarakom.

The Story

Land shaped patiently from water.

Modern Kumarakom grew from wetland reclamation around Vembanad. In the 1840s, English farmer Alfred George Baker developed hundreds of acres with canals and bunds for cultivation. Parts of the estate were left undisturbed, helping preserve the habitat that later became closely associated with Kumarakom's bird sanctuary.

Taste Kumarakom

Lake flavours and toddy-shop classics.

  • Karimeen pollichathu, pearl spot wrapped and cooked in banana leaf
  • Kuttanadan-style duck curry with appam
  • Kallappam with coconut-rich curries
  • Spicy fish curry served with tapioca
Build It In

Stay long enough to see the lake wake up.

Kumarakom works best as a calm one- or two-night backwater stop, especially in the 7-day itinerary after the hills and before the coast.
