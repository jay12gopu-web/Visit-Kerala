Alappuzha

Houseboats, canals, below-sea-level paddy fields, coir traditions, boat races, and slow village scenery.

Why Alappuzha

The classic Kerala backwater experience.

Alappuzha, also called Alleppey, lies between the Arabian Sea and a network of lakes, rivers, and canals. It is the easiest place to understand why water has shaped Kerala's agriculture, transport, food, and daily life.

The Story

A maritime town built around water.

Alappuzha's position near the sea gave it an important place in Kerala's maritime and coir history. The port town was developed under Diwan Raja Kesava Das. Traditional kettuvallams once carried rice and spices; their transformation into houseboats created the tourism experience now closely associated with the district. Snake boat racing remains a vivid expression of local water culture.
