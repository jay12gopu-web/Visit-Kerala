5-Day Plan
South Kerala Coast

Varkala & Kovalam

A dramatic sea cliff, sacred shoreline, lighthouse views, Ayurveda, surf, and unhurried beach days.

Why This Coast

Two beach towns with different rhythms.

Varkala pairs its red laterite cliff and Papanasam shore with a strong spiritual identity. Farther south, Kovalam offers three crescent-shaped beaches and an established resort atmosphere close to Thiruvananthapuram.

Famous Places

What To See Along The Coast

Varkala

Cliff & Papanasam Beach

Walk the clifftop path for Arabian Sea views, then descend to a beach that carries deep ritual and cultural importance.

Heritage

Janardhana Swami Temple

Varkala's centuries-old temple anchors the town's spiritual character. Dress respectfully and check visitor access before entering.

Kovalam

Lighthouse, Hawah & Samudra

Explore Kovalam's three linked beaches, climb toward the lighthouse view, or settle in for swimming, seafood, and sunset.

The Story

A sacred shore and a modern seaside retreat.

Varkala is often called the Southern Varanasi because of its temple traditions and the rituals associated with Papanasam Beach. Kerala Tourism records that Udaya Marthanda Varma encouraged settlement here in 1762. Kovalam's fishing coast later developed into one of Kerala's best-known beach destinations, with Lighthouse, Hawah, and Samudra forming its familiar shoreline.
